# Dimensions of Cyberspace

## Overview

Cyberspace can be understood across three interrelated dimensions. This structure helps learners move beyond treating cyber as only a technical environment.

## 1. Physical dimension

The physical layer includes the tangible infrastructure that enables cyberspace.

Examples:

- cables and fibre links
- routers and switches
- data centres
- servers and endpoints
- industrial control hardware
- satellites and communications equipment

## 2. Informational dimension

The informational layer contains the data, content, protocols, and processing activity flowing through systems.

Examples:

- records in databases
- software code
- social media posts
- emails and messages
- intelligence reports
- digital content streams

## 3. Cognitive dimension

The cognitive layer involves the people and organizations interpreting information and making decisions.

Examples:

- trust and confidence
- beliefs and assumptions
- leadership judgement
- public opinion
- operator interpretation
- institutional response

## Why this matters

Most operations touch more than one dimension. A malware event may begin in the informational layer, rely on the physical layer, and ultimately matter because of its cognitive effect on leaders, operators, or the public.
