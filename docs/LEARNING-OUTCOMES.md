# Learning Outcomes

By the end of HW401, learners should be able to:

## Knowledge outcomes

- define the fifth domain in a military and strategic context
- distinguish between the physical, informational, and cognitive dimensions of cyberspace
- explain the concepts of information dominance, decision-loop disruption, and influence propagation
- identify major categories of cyber effect including espionage, manipulation, disruption, and destruction

## Analytical outcomes

- map technical cyber activity to operational and strategic consequences
- assess how information conditions shape decision quality
- analyze influence activity as part of a broader campaign
- compare incidents using a cyber-physical-cognitive lens

## Practical outcomes

- discuss cyber incidents using defensible operational language
- interpret case studies beyond purely technical indicators
- recognize how defensive measures support resilience and continuity
- articulate how cyber actions can produce physical, informational, and cognitive outcomes simultaneously
