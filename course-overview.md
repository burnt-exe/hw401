# Course Overview

## Course title

**HW401 — Warfare in the Fifth Domain**

## Course description

HW401 examines cyber conflict as a modern battlespace where technical systems, information flows, human perception, and strategic decision-making intersect. The course introduces learners to the fifth domain as more than just networks and malware. It frames cyberspace as an operational environment in which information can be contested, cognition can be influenced, and physical systems can be affected through digital action.

## Course intent

The course is designed to help learners connect technical cyber activity to strategic outcomes. Rather than treating incidents as isolated hacks, HW401 explores how campaigns shape advantage through information superiority, disruption of adversary decision cycles, influence operations, and control over digital infrastructure.

## Audience

This course is suitable for:

- cybersecurity learners and practitioners
- defence and intelligence students
- instructors delivering cyber conflict education
- policy and strategy learners
- technical professionals needing strategic context

## Delivery profile

HW401 can be delivered as:

- instructor-led classroom training
- blended learning
- executive briefing series
- workshop-based seminar
- modular self-study sequence

## Core knowledge areas

1. Cyberspace as a domain of conflict  
2. The physical, informational, and cognitive dimensions  
3. Information superiority and operational advantage  
4. Influence and perception shaping  
5. Decision-loop disruption  
6. Cyber-physical effects and case studies  
7. Strategic relevance of cyber operations

## Expected learner value

By the end of the course, learners should be able to explain not only what cyber operations do technically, but why they matter operationally, cognitively, and strategically.
