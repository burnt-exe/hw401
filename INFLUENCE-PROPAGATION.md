# Influence Propagation

## Definition

Influence propagation is the spread of narratives, cues, messages, emotional signals, or falsehoods through connected audiences in order to shape interpretation and behaviour.

## Why it matters

Connected societies process information socially. People often decide what is true by watching others, trusting familiar voices, and reacting emotionally before they verify facts.

## Mechanisms

Influence spreads through:

- repetition
- amplification by trusted nodes
- platform algorithms
- emotional framing
- social proof
- coordinated messaging

## Operational effects

Influence propagation can:

- change public opinion
- erode trust in institutions
- increase panic or apathy
- create confusion about reality
- shift political or military pressure

## Classroom phrase

> The message is not powerful only because it exists. It becomes powerful when it spreads, sticks, and changes behaviour.
